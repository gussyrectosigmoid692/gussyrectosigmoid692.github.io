🎙️ murmur - Private voice typing for Windows

Download murmur for Windows

What is this?

Murmur turns your voice into text on your computer. You hold a button, speak, and the words appear in your chosen program. It works inside browsers, word processors, and chat apps. Your audio stays on your machine. You do not send data to the cloud. You do not pay monthly fees.

Key Benefits

  • Privacy: No audio leaves your computer.
  • Offline: It works without internet access.
  • Speed: It uses your graphics card for fast results.
  • Integration: It pastes text into any active window.

⚙️ System Requirements

Your computer needs these items to run the software smoothly:

  • Operating System: Windows 10 or 11.
  • Processor: Modern processor with at least 4 cores.
  • Memory: 8GB of system RAM.
  • Graphics: An NVIDIA GPU helps performance significantly, though it runs on the CPU if necessary.
  • Storage: 2GB of free space.

📥 How to Install

Follow these steps to setup the voice dictation tool:

  1. Go to the official download page.
  2. Find the latest version release.
  3. Download the installer file ending in .exe.
  4. Double-click the file to start the installation.
  5. Follow the prompts on your screen.
  6. The software creates a shortcut on your desktop.

🚀 How to Use

Once you open the software, it sits in your system tray at the bottom right of your screen. To dictate:

  1. Click into the text box where you want to type.
  2. Press and hold the designated push-to-talk key.
  3. Speak clearly into your microphone.
  4. Release the key.
  5. The software types the words into your application.

🛠️ Settings

Right-click the murmur icon in your taskbar to view options. You can change your push-to-talk key here. You can also tell the app to launch when windows starts so it is always ready to use. Check the history tab to see previous transcripts.

Troubleshooting

If the app does not hear your voice, check your Windows sound settings. Make sure your microphone is the default input device. Ensure your graphics card drivers are current if you want the best performance.
